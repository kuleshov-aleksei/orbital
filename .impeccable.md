## Design Context

### Users
Primary: Gaming communities and friend groups (5-10 people) who want a simple, Discord-like voice chat without the complexity or overhead of larger platforms. Users are likely Discord-familiar and expect a recognizable voice channel paradigm. They want quick setup, reliable voice quality via LiveKit, and a familiar chat UI experience.

Secondary: Small remote teams or hobbyist groups seeking minimal-setup voice communication.

### Brand Personality
**Discord-like (modern gaming)** - The app mimics Discord's familiar sidebar navigation, voice channel paradigm, and user presence indicators. Clean, modern, and functional with a gaming-adjacent aesthetic that feels familiar to the target audience.

Three-word personality: **Familiar, Reliable, Streamlined**

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: Discord-inspired dark mode-first design with modern gaming aesthetic
- **References**: Discord - familiar sidebar layout, voice rooms, user avatars with status indicators, muted/deafened states
- **Anti-references**: Enterprise software (Slack, Teams), generic AI-generated UIs, overly complex collaboration tools
- **Theme**: Multi-theme support with dark mode variants (default dark, true black, retrowave, catppuccin, solarized). Light mode NOT required - dark themes only with user-selectable variants

### Design Principles
1. **Familiar UX** - Follow Discord patterns for navigation, room lists, and user presence so users feel at home immediately
2. **Voice-First** - Audio quality and reliability are paramount; UI should get out of the way during calls
3. **Dark & Tuned** - Only dark themes; prioritize contrast and readability for long sessions
4. **Clean & Focused** - No feature bloat; every element serves a purpose in the voice chat workflow
5. **Responsive** - Works on desktop and mobile, but optimize for desktop-first voice experience
